Mark Husey

Organist, Conductor, Accompanist, & Tenor

Biography

     Award-winning and critically-acclaimed American organist Mark Husey [hju zi] has served as Director of Music & Liturgy at historic Saint Peter's Catholic Church in Columbia, South Carolina since January 2006. His two YouTube channels (YouTube.com/VisitStPeters and YouTube.com/MarkHusey) have over 2.7 million views combined. Over 700 videos of sacred choral and organ music include dozens of online premier recordings and have brought the parish's three manual 36 rank Peragallo pipe organ and intergenerational choral ministry to a global audience. Former District Convener of South Carolina AGO, he conducted the East Coast premier of James MacMillan's "Tu es petrus" with his combined professional adult and children's choirs and Australian organist Nicole Marane in the opening convocation of the 2013 Region IV Convention of the American Guild of Organists.
     Recipient of numerous awards and scholarships from Westminster Choir College and Yale, he won first prizes in the 1991 MTNA-Wurlitzer Organ competition in Miami, and the 1993 Baltimore-Washington AGO Young Artists Competition.

     He has appeared as visiting vocal coach, tenor soloist, and accompanist with the opera and choral departments at the University of South Carolina School of Music, most notably in their Concert Choir's 2008 Olympic Tour of China, performing in Beijing, Xi'an, and Shanghai. He previously served on the faculty rosters of the University of Miami and Florida International University, and was accompanist and tenor with Florida Grand Opera's Education Outreach and Chorus.
     With additional performances in Canada, England, Wales, Italy, Belgium, the Netherlands, Sweden, Finland, Estonia, and the Czech Republic, he was the first American-born organist and choral clinician to perform and lead workshops in Southwest Russia under the sponsorship of the State Philharmonia on Caucasus Mineral Waters. A tenor in small ensembles of the Grammy-winning Atlanta Symphony Orchestra Chorus and Chamber Chorus, he is one of the few organists ever reviewed in Opera News. His "Saint Jude Fanfare" has been performed internationally, most notably at a solemn pontifical nuptial Mass in Vatican City.
